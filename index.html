Inflation

Inflation can negatively impact a country's GDP by eroding purchasing power, reducing consumer spending, increasing production costs, and creating uncertainty in the economy.

Labor Force

A skilled and productive labor force positively impacts a country's GDP by driving economic growth, increasing production capacity, attracting investments, and contributing to innovation and competitiveness.

Population ages

The population ages can have various impacts on a country, including changes in workforce dynamics, healthcare demands, social security systems, and economic productivity. It can also influence the need for policies and services related to elderly care and retirement planning.

Population total

The total population of a country can impact its GDP through factors such as consumer spending, labor force size, market potential, and economic productivity, influencing overall economic growth and development.

Unemployment

Unemployment can have negative effects on a country's GDP, including reduced consumer spending, lower tax revenues, increased social welfare costs, and potential economic stagnation or recession. It can also lead to social and economic inequalities and hinder overall economic development.

Foreign direct investment

Foreign direct investment (FDI) positively impacts GDP by fostering economic growth, job creation, technology transfer, productivity enhancement, and infrastructure development, driving overall economic development and competitiveness.

Gross capital formation

Gross capital formation positively impacts a country's GDP by driving economic growth, increasing productive capacity, fostering innovation, and improving infrastructure, leading to long-term development and higher living standards.
