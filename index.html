Inflation

Inflation can negatively impact a country's GDP by eroding purchasing power, reducing consumer spending, increasing production costs, and creating uncertainty in the economy.

Labor Force

A skilled and productive labor force positively impacts a country's GDP by driving economic growth, increasing production capacity, attracting investments, and contributing to innovation and competitiveness.

Population ages

The population ages can have various impacts on a country, including changes in workforce dynamics, healthcare demands, social security systems, and economic productivity. It can also influence the need for policies and services related to elderly care and retirement planning.

Population total

The total population of a country can impact its GDP through factors such as consumer spending, labor force size, market potential, and economic productivity, influencing overall economic growth and development.

Unemployment

Unemployment can have negative effects on a country's GDP, including reduced consumer spending, lower tax revenues, increased social welfare costs, and potential economic stagnation or recession. It can also lead to social and economic inequalities and hinder overall economic development.

Foreign direct investment

Foreign direct investment (FDI) positively impacts GDP by fostering economic growth, job creation, technology transfer, productivity enhancement, and infrastructure development, driving overall economic development and competitiveness.

Gross capital formation

Gross capital formation positively impacts a country's GDP by driving economic growth, increasing productive capacity, fostering innovation, and improving infrastructure, leading to long-term development and higher living standards.

Details for the period
1996-2021

Vietnam GDP Overview

+ Vietnam's GDP from 1996 to 2021 grew relatively evenly and there was no decrease in any year.
+ From 1996 to 1997, there was a very high GDP growth rate, the average threshold was about 8.7%.
+ From 1998 to 2019, Vietnam had a stable GDP growth rate of nearly 6%/year. In particular, in the period 2016 - 2019 GDP grew at an average rate of 7%/year
+ Although 2020 and 2021 are heavily affected by the Covid-19 epidemic, Vietnam's economy still grows by an average of 2.7%.

Census

+ The population proportion of Vietnam's age group 0-14 has been gradually decreasing from 1996 to 2021 due to the implementation of family planning policies aimed at restraining population growth.
+ The population proportion of Vietnam's age group 15-64 has been gradually increasing from 1996 onwards until it stabilizes without significant growth, and even experiences a slight decline after 2008.
+ The population proportion of Vietnam's age group 65 and above has been gradually increasing from 1996 to 2021 due to economic development and improvements in education.

Population, Labor Force, Unemployment

+ Vietnam's population grows very steadily, from 75 million people in 1996 to 97.5 million people in 2021.
+ Vietnam's population tends to be younger, as evidenced by the large proportion of the population of working age, over 50% of the total population and the tendency to increase gradually from 1996 to 2019.
+ The increase in population, the proportion of the population in the working age, has steadily increased the GDP per capita as well as the total GDP of Vietnam.

Indicators

+ Foreign direct investment (FDI) accounts for a small portion (<10%) of the total investment. Generally, throughout the years, the amount of FDI has remained low and has not exceeded 10% of the total GDP.
+ The gross capital formation contributes approximately one-third of the country's total GDP. It reached its peak at 39.6% in 2007 and remained stable until 2010. Afterward, it slightly decreased but remained stable until 2021.
+ Trade: The trend in trade has been unstable, with sudden increases and decreases. It reached its peak in 2007 when Vietnam joined the World Trade Organization (WTO), and it hit a low point in 2010 due to the global financial crisis, which affected the global economy as a whole.
+ Inflation has experienced an unstable situation, reaching its peak in 2008 and a negative value in 2000.
+ The total GDP of Vietnam has consistently increased from 1996 (Total GDP: 24,657,470,575) to 2021 (366,137,590,601), demonstrating remarkable economic growth.

Population, Unemployment with GDP

+ After using linear regression, the variables that are correlated with GDP (current US$) are Population, Labor Force, Unemployment and Trade.
+ Population and labor force have a strong positive correlation with GDP. The regression results indicate that the R-Squared value for both of these variables is around 0.9, which means that this linear regression model explains about 90% of the variation in GDP.
+ Trade also has a positive correlation with GDP. This can be explained by the fact that when the trade-to-GDP ratio of a country increases, it indicates a higher level of international trade activities, expanding the market opportunities and earning more foreign currency with a higher conversion value, thereby contributing to GDP growth.
+ The unemployment rate within the labor force has a negative correlation with GDP.

Three Growthest Year

+ The three highest GDP growth years in Vietnam from 1996 to 2021 are 1996, 1997, and 2005.
+ During the period from 1992 to 1997, the economic growth rate increased to 8.77% per year, which was double the rate compared to the previous period. Inflation also decreased significantly. This is why 1996 and 1997 had very high GDP growth rates.
+ The economic growth continued to increase from 2002 to 2007, thanks to new policies implemented. In 2005, the highest GDP growth rate was recorded within the period from 2002 to 2007.
